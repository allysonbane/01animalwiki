Great Pyrenees

Description

Great Pyrenees, is a large breed of dog used as a livestock guard dog from the Pyrenees Mountains. Males grow to 110-130 pounds, while females reach 90-115 pounds.
The average lifespan is 10- 11 years. Their weather-resistant coat consists of a long, thick, flat, outer coat, where the undercoat is thin and dense.
They need very little grooming, and the outercoat doesn't mat. The breeds coat color is mainly white, but hints of grey, brown, tan, and yellow.
Even though they were bred to be hearding dogs, they can be a lazy breed. So because of this, they need 20-40 minutes of exercise daily. The breed is very smart.
It's use to handeling things by themselves. Even if they are in a dangerous situation, this breed tends to find a solution to fix it.

History

The Great Pyrenees were bred centuries ago to work in the Pyrenees Mountains between France and Spain. Although there was history of them in the 1600’s, in 1931
Mary Crane founded the breed in America. in 1931, her company Basquaerie Great Pyrenees imported the first Great Pyrenees from France. They imported nearly 60
Great Pyrenees, representing more than 10 distinct bloodlines. Fossil remains of dogs similar to Great Pyrs have been found in Bronze Age deposits dating back
from 1800 to 1000 B.C. The eighteenth century, the Great Pyrenees was very appreciated and loved by the French nobility. In 1675, this dog breed was declared by the
Dauphin from King Louis XIV’s court, the Royal Dog of France even if not long ago it was owned just by peasants. After gaining this certification, this dog type became
even more popular and has become increasingly sought by the nobles of France, who preferred them as guarding dogs for their mansions.

Temperament

Great Pyrenees are strong-willed, independent, and reserved breed. They are also attentive, quite fearless and loyal to its duties. The size of these dogs, make it an
imposing guardian. If left off a leash, they will wander off. They can be slow to learn new commands, and somewhat stubborn to train. Great Pyrs can be a wonderful
companion if you live in a suburban or rural area. Exposure to a lot of people, especially when a puppy, will help oderate any excessive protectiveness. They seem like an
intimidating breed because of their size and attitude, but very kind dogs.

Relationship With Humans

Great Pyrennes are confident, gentle, and affectionate, especially with children. This breed is ideal for assisted therapy in orphanages
and for the eldery in nursing homes or assisted living homes.
